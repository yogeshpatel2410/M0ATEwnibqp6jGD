What is Foreign Body Removal?

Foreign body removal refers to the medical procedure of extracting objects that have been accidentally swallowed or lodged in the gastrointestinal (GI) tract. These objects may include food particles, coins, buttons, or sharp items like bones, which can cause discomfort, obstruction, or serious complications if not removed promptly.  

When is Foreign Body Removal Needed?

Foreign body removal is required when objects become stuck in the GI tract, particularly in areas such as:

  • Esophagus: Common site for food or small objects.
  • Stomach: Where items may lodge after ingestion.
  • Small Intestine: Rare but potentially serious blockages.

Common Symptoms Indicating the Need for Removal

  • Difficulty swallowing or painful swallowing.
  • Drooling or inability to swallow saliva.
  • Chest or throat discomfort.
  • Abdominal pain or bloating.
  • Vomiting or nausea.
  • Coughing or choking episodes.

Types of Foreign Bodies Commonly Removed

  • Food Bolus: Pieces of food that become lodged in the esophagus.
  • Non-Food Objects: Coins, buttons, batteries, toys, or sharp items like bones or pins.
  • Medical Devices: Malfunctioning feeding tubes or dislodged stents.

How is Foreign Body Removal Performed?

At Kaizen Care, we use advanced endoscopic techniques for safe and efficient foreign body removal:  

  1. Preparation:
    Patients may need to fast for a few hours before the procedure.
    Sedation or anesthesia is administered for patient comfort.
  2. Procedure:
    An endoscope, a thin and flexible tube with a camera, is gently inserted through the mouth into the esophagus and stomach.
    Specialized tools are used to grasp or dislodge the foreign object for removal.
    If sharp or risky objects are involved, utmost care is taken to avoid injury.
  3. Recovery:
    The procedure is usually quick, and patients can return home the same day.
    Follow-up care is provided to monitor for any residual discomfort or complications.

FAQs About Foreign Body Removal

Is Foreign Body Removal painful?

No, the procedure is performed under sedation or anesthesia, ensuring a painless experience.

How long does the procedure take?

The procedure typically lasts 15–30 minutes, depending on the location and type of foreign object.

Are there risks associated with foreign body removal?

While complications are rare, risks may include minor bleeding or injury to the GI tract. These risks are minimized when performed by skilled specialists.

What should I do if I suspect a foreign object is stuck?

Seek immediate medical attention. Avoid eating or drinking until the object is evaluated by a healthcare professional.
