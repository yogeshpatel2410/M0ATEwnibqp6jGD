What â€‹is a Colonoscopy?

A colonoscopy is a minimally invasive procedure used to examine the interior of the large intestine (colon) and rectum. Using a thin, flexible tube called a colonoscope equipped with a camera, doctors can identify abnormalities, take biopsies, and perform therapeutic interventions.  

Why is a Colonoscopy Performed?

A colonoscopy is an essential diagnostic and preventive tool recommended for:

  • Screening for colon cancer or polyps.
  • Investigating symptoms such as:
    - Persistent abdominal pain.
    - Chronic diarrhea or constipation.
    - Rectal bleeding.
    - Unexplained weight loss.
  • Diagnosing conditions like:
    - Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD): Crohnâ€™s disease or ulcerative colitis.
    - Diverticulosis or diverticulitis.
    - Colonic strictures or obstructions.

Conditions Diagnosed or Treated with Colonoscopy

  • Colon Cancer and Polyps: Early detection and removal of precancerous growths.
  • Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD): Monitoring disease activity and treatment effectiveness.
  • Colonic Bleeding: Identifying and managing sources of gastrointestinal bleeding.
  • Diverticulosis: Evaluating and managing complications like inflammation or infection.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS): Ruling out other conditions contributing to symptoms.

How is a Colonoscopy Performed?

At Kaizen Care, we follow a standardized process to ensure patient safety and comfort:  

  1. Preparation:
    Patients are given specific dietary instructions and a bowel-cleansing regimen to ensure a clear view of the colon.
    Sedation or anesthesia may be provided for relaxation and comfort.
  2. Procedure:
    The colonoscope is gently inserted through the rectum and guided through the colon.
    The camera transmits detailed images, allowing for a thorough examination.
    Biopsies can be taken, and polyps or other abnormalities removed during the procedure.
  3. Recovery:
    Patients are observed for a short period post-procedure to ensure there are no complications.
    Normal activities can usually resume the following day.

FAQs About Colonoscopy

Is Colonoscopy painful?

Most patients experience minimal discomfort during the procedure, especially when sedation or anesthesia is used.

How long does the procedure take?

The procedure typically takes 30â€“60 minutes, but plan for a few hours including preparation and recovery.

How often should I have a colonoscopy?

For average-risk individuals, colonoscopy screenings are recommended every 10 years starting at age 45. Your doctor may recommend more frequent screenings based on your medical history.

What should I avoid after a colonoscopy?

Avoid heavy meals, alcohol, and strenuous activities for the rest of the day following the procedure.
