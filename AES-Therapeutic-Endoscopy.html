What is Therapeutic Endoscopy?

Therapeutic endoscopy refers to minimally invasive procedures performed using an endoscope to treat various gastrointestinal (GI) conditions. Unlike diagnostic endoscopy, which focuses on identifying issues, therapeutic endoscopy allows for direct interventions to resolve them without the need for open surgery.  

Why is Therapeutic Endoscopy Performed?

Therapeutic endoscopy is used to treat various conditions of the digestive tract, such as:

  • Stopping GI bleeding.
  • Removing polyps or tumors.
  • Treating strictures or blockages.
  • Managing complications of pancreatitis or bile duct disorders.
  • Placing stents to maintain the patency of ducts or tracts.

Conditions Treated with Therapeutic Endoscopy

  • Gastrointestinal Bleeding: Stopping bleeding using techniques like banding, sclerotherapy, or argon plasma coagulation.
  • Esophageal Varices: Preventing or controlling bleeding from dilated veins in the esophagus.
  • Polyps and Tumors: Removing benign polyps or early-stage tumors.
  • Strictures and Blockages: Dilating narrowed areas in the esophagus, stomach, or intestines.
  • Bile Duct Stones or Blockages: Removing stones or placing stents to restore bile flow.
  • Pseudocysts or Fluid Collections: Draining pancreatic or abdominal fluid collections.

Therapeutic Endoscopy Procedures Offered at Kaizen Care

  1. Endoscopic Band Ligation:

  2. Polypectomy:
    Removing benign polyps in the colon or stomach to prevent cancer progression.
  3. Endoscopic Dilatation:

  4. Stent Placement:
    Inserting stents to open blocked bile ducts, esophagus, or other passages.
  5. ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography):
    Managing bile and pancreatic duct disorders, including stone removal and stent placement.
  6. Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS)-Guided Procedures:
    Performing biopsies, drainage, or targeted injections with precision.
  7. Argon Plasma Coagulation (APC):
    Controlling bleeding or removing abnormal tissue with precision.

FAQs About Therapeutic Endoscopy

Is therapeutic endoscopy painful?

No, most procedures are performed under sedation or anesthesia to ensure a painless experience.

How long does the therapeutic endoscopy procedure take?

The duration varies depending on the type of procedure but generally ranges from 30 minutes to 2 hours.

Are there risks associated with therapeutic endoscopy?

While therapeutic endoscopy is safe, rare risks include bleeding, infection, or perforation. These are minimized when performed by experienced specialists.

How soon can I return to normal activities after the procedure?

Most patients can resume normal activities within 24 hours, depending on the complexity of the procedure.
